Throughout my college career, I have found that my fall semesters are hard and my springs are long. Each fall is harder than the last. This being my senior year during a pandemic, its going to be a record breaker again. My hopes after this year are to go to graduate school, so I have (yet) to study for the GRE and take the test. I am pursuing an honor's thesis so I will be doing extracurricular research and meetings outside of class. I am essentially learning two new languages, R, and Stata. My two major elective classes have a lot of reading and tedious work that takes a lot out of my weeks. And on top of all of that, I also try to work a few hours a week at my job that I actually enjoy to an extent. In the summer it was the same group of us working every day. Now, all the new people and those that went home for the summer are back. I miss the slow pace of the summer as well as the lack of traffic and growing COVID cases. I miss seeing my friend every day, but now we're mostly too busy for each other. I hope I can survive this semester, and well enough to have two more fall semesters as a student.
